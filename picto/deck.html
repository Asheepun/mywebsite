Fresh and Innovative Gameplay

Picto is a game about using your phone's camera to interact with the real world. The game will ask you questions, in the form of levels, and it's up to you to find the answer somewhere in your surroundings.

How will you get over that gap? How will you make it past that guard dog? How will you escape through that solid wall? You are allowed to use your full creativity, to find an answer somewhere in the infinite sandbox of life.

There are many different ways to get around your foes!

The game aims to explore the full consequence of mixing reality with a video game, and does not shy away from all of the amazing things that can happen, but rather embraces them. You can walk around on your shadow or your reflection in the mirror. You can create the solution to a level with objects in your room, find it out in nature, draw it with pen and paper or on a computer screen.

Blend colors to phase through walls between life and the game.

Custom Made Game Engine

The game's engine is written from scratch in C++ and uses a new mixed reality technique not seen in any previous game. The idea behind the tech is to create as much interesting gameplay as possible, rather than making the most immersive AR experience, which might be what gives it the edge over traditional AR games.

Rendering is done with OpenGL on Android and Metal on iOS. The game interfaces with the phone's native camera and gyroscope APIs directly, and does not rely on any external SDKs or libraries.

Picto is way more performant than other AR game that use AR-core. It runs at 60 fps on an iPhone 7 (from 2018), which makes it feel way more fun and premium to play. The startup and load time is a few 100 milliseconds, which is way faster than almost every other mobile game, AR or not. This is thanks to lots of dedicated optimization work.

We believe that software quality matters a lot, and we're turning Picto into a fast and robust application.

On top of the mixed reality tech, Picto can simultaneously run a real time semi-eulerian fluid simulation with up to 32000 particles at 60 fps (also on the iPhone 7).

In some levels, blue pixels turn into water that Tim can swim in.

A Story About What's Actually Going On

The game's story is based on observing what's actually going while you're playing the game. The main character is called Tim, and he lives inside of a video game, but he does not know this. When you play the game, Tim gets to run around in a projection of our universe, and the question is, how does he feel about this?

What would it be like if you all of a sudden appeared inside of a world infinitely more complex than your own? If you suddenly got picked up by the hand of a 4-dimensional being?

On your adventure, you will encounter other people living in the game world. Such as a monk who believes that humans are gods, or a scientist who's trying to discover the laws of physics, which in this case is the game's code.

Picto's story won't be told directly, but will instead be hidden in the game's art and through short written notes. There will be lots of secrets for players to find and piece together, similar to games like Hollow Knight and Animal Well.

Some levels take place in a 3D world, simulated on a mad scientists computer.

A Level Editor - IRL Mario Maker

In addition to the main adventure and puzzle game, Picto also features a super mario maker style level editor. However, instead of placing tiles inside the game, you draw/build the level in real life and take a picture/video of it. You can then place out game objects such as players, stars and enemies, that automatically will interract with your drawings.

A level can be composed of multiple pictures and videos, and "action buttons" can be placed out to switch between them. Action buttons give you the same sort of gameplay logic as a choose-your-own-adventure book, and can be put over anything that you draw. This lets Picto levels have interractable doors, levers, switches, npc dialogue, or anything you can imagine!

Demonstration of using action buttons to create a door.

We've added more mechanics since this video was filmed, and we're planning on improving the editor by testing it a lot, and working closely with the game's community to figure out what they want to be able to do.

Levels can easily be exported to a custom file format ".pictoLevel" and sent to your friends. You can then just click on the file on your phone, and Picto will open with that level loaded. This makes it so that we don't have to store levels on a centralized server, and lets us avoid all of the problems that arise when you become responsible for storing people's photos and videos.

Business Model

Our vision is to release Picto as a premium game with no ads or micro-transactions. We believe that this is the way games should work in an ideal world, and thus, this is how we want to monetize it.

Due to the public reception we've gotten, and the game's eye catching visuals, we think people will be willing to buy it. We also believe people will be extra happy when playing the game, since it will respect them and won't try to Skinner box them for cash constantly.

While developing Picto, one of our inspirations has been the game Monument Valley, since it stands out as an artistic, premium, mobile puzzle game that did really well (over 10 million copies sold). It cost $3.99, and we think this is a reasonable price for Picto as well. Potentially slightly higher due to inflation.

Release Plan & Development Progress

Picto is over 90% completed, and is going to be in a shippable state this summer. However, we don't want to release it until we have a proper marketing strategy in place. We're also thinking about doing an early access release before the summer to work on features such as the level editor with lots of user feedback.

Closed testing of the full game will begin with a small group of people from the game's discord server in february 2026.
